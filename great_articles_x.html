Influential Papers in Statistical Probability

This collection features a selection of influential papers in statistical probability that have significantly contributed to the field. These articles delve into various aspects of statistical theory, methods, and applications, providing valuable insights for researchers, practitioners, and enthusiasts. Explore these seminal works to deepen your understanding of statistical probability.

Cohen, J. (1994). "The Earth is Round (p < .05)". American Psychologist.

Summary: Cohen's paper discusses the over-reliance on p-values in scientific research and emphasizes the need for a more nuanced interpretation of statistical significance.

DOI: 10.1037/0003-066X.49.12.997

Gigerenzer, G. (2004). "Mindless Statistics."

Summary: Gigerenzer challenges traditional statistical methods and argues for more intuitive and transparent approaches to statistical reasoning.

DOI: 10.1017/cbo9780511808098.003

Taleb, N.N. (2007). "The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable."

Summary: Taleb explores the concept of rare and unpredictable events and their profound influence on our lives, providing insights into risk management and decision-making.

DOI: 10.1002/9780470390171

Tukey, J. W. (1977). "Exploratory Data Analysis."

Summary: Tukey advocates for visual and exploratory approaches to data analysis, emphasizing the importance of graphical techniques in understanding datasets.

DOI: 10.1007/978-1-4612-1096-3

Neyman, J., Pearson, E. S. (1933). "On the Problem of the Most Efficient Tests of Statistical Hypotheses." Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London. Series A, Containing Papers of a Mathematical or Physical Character.

Summary: This foundational work introduces the concept of hypothesis testing and provides insights into the development of optimal statistical tests.

DOI: 10.1098/rsta.1933.0009

Fisher, R. A. (1925). "Statistical Methods for Research Workers."

Summary: Fisher's classic book introduces many fundamental statistical concepts and techniques widely used in research.

DOI: 10.1002/bimj.4710100504

Student (1908). "The Probable Error of a Mean." Biometrika.

Summary: This influential paper, authored under the pseudonym 'Student', introduces the concept of the t-test, a widely used statistical test for comparing means.

DOI: 10.2307/2331554

Bayes, T. (1763). "An Essay towards solving a Problem in the Doctrine of Chances." Philosophical Transactions.

Summary: Bayes' Theorem, introduced in this paper, revolutionized the field of Bayesian statistics, providing a framework for updating beliefs based on new evidence.

DOI: 10.1098/rstl.1763.0053

Kolmogorov, A. N. (1933). "Foundations of the Theory of Probability."

Summary: Kolmogorov's seminal work formalizes probability theory, establishing axioms and fundamental concepts that underpin modern probability theory.

DOI: 10.2307/1989333

Kendall, M., & Stuart, A. (1967). "The Advanced Theory of Statistics." (Vol. 1).

Summary: This comprehensive textbook covers a wide range of statistical methods, providing a solid foundation in statistical theory and practice.

DOI: 10.1002/9780470316669
