Descriptive Statistics: Measures of Central Tendency

by SELVASTICS

Measures of Central Tendency

Measures of central tendency describe the center of a dataset. The three most commonly used measures of central tendency are the mean, median, and mode.

Mean

The mean is the average of all the values in the dataset. It is calculated using the formula:

\(\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_i\)

The mean is sensitive to outliers, which can skew the value. In some cases, the median may be a better measure of central tendency.

Median

The median is the middle value when the data is arranged in order. It is calculated using the formula:

\(med = \begin{cases} x_{(n+1)/2} & \text{if } n \text{ is odd} \\ \frac{1}{2}(x_{n/2} + x_{n/2+1}) & \text{if } n \text{ is even} \end{cases}\)

The median is less sensitive to outliers than the mean, making it a better measure of central tendency in some cases.

Mode

he mode is the most common value in the dataset. It is the value that occurs with the highest frequency.

The mode is useful for describing the most typical value in a dataset, but it may not be a good measure of

central tendency if the dataset has multiple modes or if the values are continuous.
